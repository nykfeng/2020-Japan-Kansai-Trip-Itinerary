Taxis in Kyoto

Basic Taxi Etiquette & Payment

  • On most streets hailing a taxi is easy. Just raise your hand for an oncoming taxi as you would in your home country. If the light in the passengerâ€™s side window is green, the taxi is already occupied. If the light is red, then the taxi is available. At night, a light on the roof of the taxi also indicates that the taxi is vacant.
  • The majority of taxi drivers cannot speak English. Itâ€™s a good idea to show them a map to your destination, or to have the address written down in Japanese.
  • The rear doors of taxi cabs open and close automatically. You donâ€™t need to open and close them yourself, in fact it is better if you donâ€™t.
  • All taxi cabs have meters, and taxi drivers are generally honest, so you donâ€™t need to worry about being cheated.
  • There is no need to tip in Japan. You simply pay the fare shown on the meter. If you try to tip the driver, you will probably just embarrass or confuse him.
  • Not all taxis accept credit cards, so it is best to have the appropriate amount ready to pay in cash. If you wish to pay with a credit card, you should check that your card is acceptable with the driver before the journey begins.

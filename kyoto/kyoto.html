Kyoto 京都市

Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city on the island of Honshu. It's famous for its numerous classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines and traditional wooden houses. It’s also known for formal traditions such as kaiseki dining, consisting of multiple courses of precise dishes, and geisha, female entertainers often found in the Gion district. Over the centuries, Kyoto was destroyed by many wars and fires, but due to its exceptional historic value, the city was dropped from the list of target cities for the atomic bomb and escaped destruction during World War II. Countless temples, shrines and other historically priceless structures survive in the city today.

京都市是位于日本京都府南部的城市，为京都府府治，也是政令指定都市之一。京都市的面积为827.83平方公里，下辖11个区。2017年10月，京都市有人口 1,472,027人，是日本人口第八多的都市。

自794年桓武天皇迁都平安京到1868年明治天皇东京奠都为止，京都一直都是日本的首都。长年的历史积淀使得京都市拥有相当丰富的历史遗迹，也是日本传统 文化的重镇之一。京都的一些传统民俗祭典，诸如葵祭、祇园祭等已举办超过千年，成为京都独有的风景。京都市的部分历史建筑在1994年以“古都京都的文化 财”的名义被列为世界文化遗产。这些因素使得京都吸引了众多观光客，2008年造访京都的日本国内外游客数便已突破5000万人。京都也是日本重要的工业城市， 其中又以传统产业和电子产业最为重要。京都是日本最重要的文化教育城市之一，市内共有37所大学，每百万人平均拥有大学数量和学生占人口比例都在日本大 都市中居首。

🍱(錦市場)

Nishiki Market is a marketplace in downtown Kyoto, located on a road one block north and parallel to Shijō Street and west of Teramachi Street. Rich with history and tradition, the market is renowned as the place to obtain many of Kyoto's famous foods and goods.

🌿(嵐山)

One of Kyoto’s top sights and for good reason: standing amid these soaring stalks of bamboo is like being in another world. 位于京都市区以西的岚山自平安时代以来就是许多贵族的别庄所在地，名称经常出现在历史故事与古典文学 之中。除了自然景观外，岚山地区也是很多日本知名古刹神社的所在地，与多位古代日本天皇的安葬之处，因此前来此地参拜庙宇神社是游客主要的观光重点之一。

🎴Ponto-chō 先斗町

先斗町是一条窄窄的美食街。京都其他地方吃饭的店都关门很早，六七点就没人了，除了一些通宵便利店，但是这一带估计是上班族下班后常来的地方，很热闹。 顺便提一下，这边的下班时间貌似也挺晚， 九十点钟还常看见工薪阶层的男男女女结伴下班，互相鞠躬告别，小街中偶尔会有一两个和服女子匆匆路过，给雨夜增加几分风情，来不及拍。

⛩Fushimi Inari Taisha 伏見稲荷大社

伏見稻荷大社位於稻荷山的山麓，在傳統上整個稻荷山的範圍都被視為是神域的範圍。伏見稻荷大社主要是祀奉以宇迦之御魂大神為首的諸位稻荷神，自古以來就是農業與商業的神明，除此之外也配祀包括佐田彥大神、大宮能賣大神、田中大神與四大神等其他的神明。 由於每年都有大量的香客前來神社祭拜求取農作豐收、生意興隆、交通安全，使得該神社成為京都地區香火最盛的神社之一。

🎏Kiyomizu-dera 清水寺

年间超过400万人到访，在京都是深受欢迎的景点。在陡峭的悬崖上建造的悬空的正殿"清水舞台"，可将京都市景尽收眼底。春天樱花和秋天红叶的季节，在此远眺可谓景色之一绝。不同到访时间，景色亦完全不同，早晨可感受到清新的空气， 中午可随处饱览晴爽的景色，黄昏时刻那一带披上金黄色，夜间灯彩营造的梦幻空间四处延伸。

Taxis in Kyoto

Basic Taxi Etiquette & Payment

  • On most streets hailing a taxi is easy. Just raise your hand for an oncoming taxi as you would in your home country. If the light in the passenger’s side window is green, the taxi is already occupied. If the light is red, then the taxi is available. At night, a light on the roof of the taxi also indicates that the taxi is vacant.
  • The majority of taxi drivers cannot speak English. It’s a good idea to show them a map to your destination, or to have the address written down in Japanese.
  • The rear doors of taxi cabs open and close automatically. You don’t need to open and close them yourself, in fact it is better if you don’t.
  • All taxi cabs have meters, and taxi drivers are generally honest, so you don’t need to worry about being cheated.
  • There is no need to tip in Japan. You simply pay the fare shown on the meter. If you try to tip the driver, you will probably just embarrass or confuse him.
  • Not all taxis accept credit cards, so it is best to have the appropriate amount ready to pay in cash. If you wish to pay with a credit card, you should check that your card is acceptable with the driver before the journey begins.

Kyoto to Tennoji Station

Use the The Keihan Main Line – Keihan Electric Railway

Keihan Electric Railway is a private company that operates a number of rail lines in the Kyoto, Shiga and Osaka areas. None of these rail lines pass through Kyoto Station, and they are not covered by the Japan Rail Pass. However, they are very convenient when traveling between Kyoto and Osaka, or when traveling between major tourist locations in Kyoto. In this article we will focus on the Keihan Main Line which is the most useful line for visitors to Kyoto.
